# HKMA — Mandate and Functions

The Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) is the central banking institution
of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region. It was established on
1 April 1993 by merging the Office of the Exchange Fund with the Office
of the Commissioner of Banking.

## Primary objectives

The HKMA has four primary objectives set out in the Exchange Fund Ordinance:

1. To maintain currency stability within the framework of the Linked
   Exchange Rate System. The Hong Kong dollar is pegged to the US dollar
   at a rate of HK$7.80 to US$1, with permitted trading band of HK$7.75
   to HK$7.85.
2. To promote the stability and integrity of the financial system,
   including the banking system.
3. To help maintain Hong Kong's status as an international financial centre,
   including the maintenance and development of Hong Kong's financial
   infrastructure.
4. To manage the Exchange Fund.

## Fintech 2025 strategy

Launched in June 2021, Fintech 2025 is HKMA's strategy to drive fintech
development. It has five focus areas:

- All banks go fintech
- Future-proofing Hong Kong for Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC)
- Creating the next-generation data infrastructure
- Expanding the fintech-savvy workforce
- Nurturing the ecosystem with funding and policies

## e-HKD pilot

The HKMA has been conducting research and pilots on the retail Central Bank
Digital Currency (CBDC), known as e-HKD. Phase 1 of the e-HKD pilot
programme commenced in 2023, exploring use cases including programmable
payments, tokenised deposits, and settlement of tokenised assets.
